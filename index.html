Before you begin
Islam comes first. Your wasiyyah is a command from the Prophet ﷺ. It is an act of worship, valid before Allah the moment you complete it sincerely and have it witnessed. No civil law changes this obligation.

We are Muslims living in a world whose systems were not built on our deen. Some countries have civil inheritance laws that may affect how an Islamic will is applied. We acknowledge this reality, but it does not change our obligation. We write our wasiyyah because the Prophet ﷺ told us to, and we deal with civil systems separately if we must.

A note on civil law: If you want your wasiyyah to be recognised legally in your country, you may choose to have it reviewed by a local attorney after completing it here. This is optional and entirely separate from your Islamic obligation. This tool provides Islamic guidance only. Its creator bears no legal responsibility for how any civil authority handles your document.

How it works

Guided from beginning to end.

Six sections covering every aspect of an Islamic will. Clear questions, Quranic evidence at every step, and a complete document when you are done.

1
Personal declaration
Begin with your shahadah and personal details. Confirm you are of sound mind. Name your executors in order of preference in case one is unavailable.
2
Religious obligations
Before any wealth is distributed, address what you owe to Allah. Missed prayers, fasts, unpaid zakat, and any pending Hajj. These debts to Allah come before all others.
3
Rights of people
Debts you owe, money owed to you, things held in trust, and anyone you may have wronged. Leave this world with a clean slate before Allah and His creation.
4
Inheritance distribution
List your assets and let the Fara'id calculator divide them according to Shariah. Every share is cited directly from Quran and Sunnah, with blocking rules applied automatically.
5
Funeral wishes
Name who performs the ghusl, who leads the janazah, and where you wish to be buried. Your family will have clear written instructions at the hardest moment.
6
Messages to loved ones
Your final words to your spouse, children, parents, and family. Words of love, advice, and dua that will remain with them long after you have returned to Allah.

Common objections

Do I really need one?

Some common reasons Muslims delay writing their wasiyyah — and why none of them hold up.

"I'm too young."
Death has no minimum age. The Prophet ﷺ did not say write your will when you are old. He said not to let two nights pass. Youth is not a shield — it is an opportunity to prepare while you still have time and clarity.
"I don't have much."
A wasiyyah is not only about wealth. It records your religious obligations, the rights of others upon you, your funeral wishes, and your final words to your family. These matter regardless of how much you own.
"I'll do it later."
Later is not guaranteed. The Prophet ﷺ specifically warned against delay. We plan for everything except the one certainty in life. It takes 15 minutes. Do it today.
"It's too complicated."
That is exactly why this tool exists. Every field is explained, every ruling is evidenced, and the inheritance calculator handles everything automatically. Start and you will see how simple it is.
"My family already knows."
Grief changes people. Verbal agreements are forgotten, disputed, or misremembered. A written, witnessed wasiyyah removes ambiguity and protects your family from disputes at the worst possible moment.
"I'm not sure of the rulings."
Every ruling includes its evidence from Quran and authentic Sunnah. You do not need prior knowledge. The tool guides you through everything step by step with explanations at every point.

A message from the founder

Why I built this.

Personal
I built this because I was scared.

Not scared of death itself. As Muslims we know that death is a beginning, not an end. What scared me was the thought of leaving this world without my affairs in order. Without my family knowing what I wanted. Without having written down what the Prophet ﷺ told every one of us to write.

I am not a scholar. I am not a lawyer. I am an ordinary Muslim, travelling the world, sitting far from my family one evening, and asking myself what would happen if I died that night. And when I looked around, I saw that most Muslims I knew had never written a wasiyyah. Not because they did not care, but because no one had made it simple enough. And I had never written one either.

So I built this. With my own hands, one late night at a time, asking Allah to put barakah in it and accept it as an act of worship. It is free because the deen is not a product. It is private because your wasiyyah is between you and Allah. And it is built on Quran and Sunnah because that is the only foundation worth building on.

If this helps even one Muslim leave this world with their affairs in order, their family protected, and their obligations recorded, then every hour I gave to build it was worth it. That is my only intention, and I ask Allah to accept it from me and from you.
