Before you begin
Islam comes first. Your wasiyyah is a command from the Prophet ﷺ. It is not a legal document first and a religious one second. It is an act of worship, and it is valid before Allah the moment you complete it with sincerity and have it witnessed. No law of any land changes that obligation.
We are Muslims living in a world whose systems were not built on our deen. Some countries have civil inheritance laws that may complicate how an Islamic will is applied. This is a reality we acknowledge, but it does not change our obligation to Allah. We do not bend our religion to fit their laws. We write our wasiyyah because the Prophet ﷺ told us to, and we deal with civil systems separately if we have to.
A note on civil law: If you live in a country with strict inheritance regulations and you want to give your wasiyyah the best chance of being recognised legally, you may choose to have it reviewed by a local attorney after completing it here. This is entirely optional and separate from your Islamic obligation. This tool provides Islamic guidance only. Its creator bears no legal responsibility for how any civil authority handles the document you produce.

How it works

Guided from beginning to end.

Six sections covering every aspect of an Islamic will. Clear questions, Quranic evidence at every step, and a complete document when you are done.

1
Personal declaration
Begin with your shahadah, your details, and a declaration that you are of sound mind. This is the spiritual and legal foundation of your wasiyyah.
2
Religious obligations
Before anything else, address what you owe to Allah. Missed prayers, fasts, unpaid zakat, and any pending Hajj. These debts to Allah come before all others.
3
Rights of people
Debts you owe, money owed to you, things held in trust, and anyone you may have wronged. Leave this world with a clean slate before Allah and His creation.
4
Inheritance distribution
List your assets and let the Fara'id calculator divide them according to Shariah law, based on your family structure. Every share is cited from Quran and Sunnah.
5
Funeral wishes
Name who performs the ghusl, who leads the janazah prayer, and where you wish to be buried. Your body has rights over you, and the Sunnah shows exactly how they are fulfilled.
6
Messages to loved ones
Your final words to your spouse, children, parents, and family. Words of love, advice, and dua that will remain with them long after you have returned to Allah.

A message from the founder

Why I built this.

Personal
I built this because I was scared.

Not scared of death itself. As Muslims we know that death is a beginning, not an end. What scared me was the thought of leaving this world without my affairs in order. Without my family knowing what I wanted. Without having written down what the Prophet ﷺ told every one of us to write.

I am not a scholar. I am not a lawyer. I am an ordinary Muslim, travelling the world, sitting far from my family one evening, and asking myself what would happen if I died that night. And when I looked around, I saw that most Muslims I knew had never written a wasiyyah. Not because they didn't care, but because no one had made it simple enough. And I had never written one either.

So I built this. With my own hands, one late night at a time, asking Allah to put barakah in it and accept it as an act of worship. It is free because the deen is not a product. It is private because your wasiyyah is between you and Allah. And it is built on Quran and Sunnah because that is the only foundation worth building on.

If this helps even one Muslim leave this world with their affairs in order, their family protected, and their obligations recorded, then every hour I gave to build it was worth it. That is my only intention, and I ask Allah to accept it from me and from you.
