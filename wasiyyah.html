Why include the shahadah?
Beginning the wasiyyah with the shahadah is the Sunnah. It establishes clearly that this is an Islamic will written by a Muslim, and it is a beautiful and fitting way to open this document.
أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ
I testify that there is no god worthy of worship except Allah, and I testify that Muhammad is the messenger of Allah.

What is an executor?
The executor is the person you trust to carry out the instructions in your wasiyyah after you pass. They do not inherit from you — they simply oversee that your wishes are fulfilled. Name a backup in case the first is unable to serve.

Religious obligations
Before any wealth is distributed, address what you owe to Allah. These debts come before all others.
"The debt owed to Allah is most deserving to be paid."
Narrated by Ibn Abbas رضي الله عنه, from the Prophet ﷺ. Sahih Bukhari 1953.
Missed prayers (Qaza Salah) recommended
About missed prayers
Addressing missed prayers in your wasiyyah is recommended, not obligatory. If you missed obligatory prayers during your life, note an estimate here and instruct your family to give sadaqah on your behalf as a gesture of sincerity before Allah. The scholars have differed on the ruling of qaza salah after death, but making an effort to address it shows sincere repentance and concern for one's account with Allah.
Missed fasts (Qaza Sawm) recommended
About missed fasts
If you have missed Ramadan fasts without making them up, your family may fast on your behalf, or pay fidyah — feeding one poor person for each missed day from your estate. Both are established in the Sunnah.
"Whoever dies and has fasts to make up, his heir should fast on his behalf."
Narrated by Aisha رضي الله عنها, from the Prophet ﷺ. Sahih Bukhari 1952 and Sahih Muslim 1147.
Unpaid Zakat recommended
About unpaid zakat
If you believe you owe unpaid zakat from previous years, instruct your executor to pay it from your estate before distribution. Zakat is a right of the poor upon your wealth, and is treated as a debt owed to Allah.
"The debt owed to Allah is most deserving to be paid."
Narrated by Ibn Abbas رضي الله عنه, from the Prophet ﷺ. Sahih Bukhari 1953.
Hajj obligation
About the Hajj obligation
Hajj is obligatory once in a lifetime for every Muslim who is able financially and physically. If you were able but did not perform it, your executor may arrange for a Hajj badal (proxy Hajj) to be performed on your behalf from your estate.
"Hajj may be performed on behalf of the deceased."
Narrated by Ibn Abbas رضي الله عنه, from the Prophet ﷺ. Sahih Bukhari 1852.

Rights of people (Huquq al-Ibad)
These are the rights others hold over you. They are among the most serious matters a Muslim must address before leaving this world.
"Whoever has wronged his brother with regard to his honour or anything else, let him seek his pardon today, before there is no dinar or dirham — if he has good deeds, they will be taken from him equal to his wrong, and if he has no good deeds, some of his brother's sins will be loaded upon him."
Narrated by Abu Hurairah رضي الله عنه, from the Prophet ﷺ. Sahih Bukhari 2449.
If you have wronged anyone in this life — whether in wealth, in words, in action, or in anything else — go and address it now, while you are still alive. Do not leave it for your family to handle after you are gone. Seeking forgiveness while alive is far better and is what the Prophet ﷺ commanded.

Funeral and burial wishes
Your funeral instructions will be included in your wasiyyah document, protecting your family from making decisions that contradict the Sunnah at a time of grief.
"Hasten the funeral. If the deceased was righteous, you are sending him forward to good. And if other than that, you are removing evil from your necks."
Narrated by Abu Hurairah رضي الله عنه, from the Prophet ﷺ. Sahih Bukhari 1315 and Sahih Muslim 944.
Included automatically in every document
The following binding instructions are part of every wasiyyah produced by this tool, because they reflect obligations established in the Quran and Sunnah. They are not optional, and they will appear in your document regardless of other choices:

No wailing or loud lamenting. Patience and submission to Allah's decree is obligatory.
No innovations (bid'ah) in the funeral proceedings. Follow the Sunnah only.
No extravagance in burial. Simplicity is the Sunnah and the right of the deceased.
No mixing of men and women at the funeral gathering.
No photographs or videos of the deceased.
Who should perform the ghusl recommended
About the ghusl
The ghusl (ritual washing of the body) must be performed by someone of the same gender as the deceased, except for spouses. Naming your preferences removes this burden from your family at a time of grief. List up to three people in order of preference.

Sadaqah Jariyah recommended
Give now, not just after death
The best sadaqah is the one you give while you are alive and healthy. Do not delay charity thinking your wasiyyah will cover it — give whatever you are able to now. What you record here is simply a request to your family for after your passing.
"The best charity is that given when you are healthy and in need of it yourself, fearing poverty and hoping for wealth."
Narrated by Abu Hurairah رضي الله عنه, from the Prophet ﷺ. Sahih Bukhari 1419.
Give in charity now while you are alive if you are able to. What you write below is a request to your family to continue charity on your behalf after your passing.

Important — review your wasiyyah every year
Your wasiyyah is saved on this device and can be updated at any time. You should return to review and update it after any major life change such as marriage, the birth of a child, a death in the family, or a significant change in your assets. Even without major changes, reviewing it once a year is a good habit and takes only a few minutes. A wasiyyah that no longer reflects your circumstances may not represent your true wishes.
